Bakunawa


Overview: The Bakunawa is a fearsome sea serpent in Philippine mythology. It is often said to be responsible for eclipses, as it is believed to swallow the moon. The Bakunawa is considered one of the most terrifying creatures in Filipino folklore.

P hysical Description

  • Large sea serpent, often depicted with a body like a dragon
  • Its scales glimmer like the night sky
  • It has a long, sinuous body, with sharp teeth and glowing eyes

Abilities

  • Ability to control the tides and cause storms
  • Can swallow the moon, causing eclipses
  • Has the power to create fear and darkness when it is in the process of swallowing the moon

Weaknesses

  • Its power can be broken by loud noises or music, which is said to scare it away
  • Sometimes, people use offerings or prayers to appease the Bakunawa
  • Religious symbols and the light of the moon can also weaken it

Cultural Significance

The Bakunawa is a major part of Filipino folklore and is often associated with natural phenomena like eclipses. In some traditions, it is also seen as a guardian of the seas. The myth of the Bakunawa reflects the people's awe and respect for the natural world and its mysteries.
