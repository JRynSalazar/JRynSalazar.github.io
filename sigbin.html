Sigbin


Overview: The Sigbin is a mythical creature from the Visayas and Mindanao regions of the Philippines. It is believed to come out at night to suck the blood of victims from their shadows. The Sigbin is both feared and revered, and some say powerful families secretly keep them as pets for wealth and protection.

P hysical Description

  • Resembles a hornless goat or kangaroo with large ears
  • Walks backwards with its head lowered between its hind legs
  • Has a foul odor and glowing red eyes

Abilities

  • Can turn invisible to humans
  • Feeds by sucking the blood from victims' shadows
  • Moves silently and quickly, making it nearly impossible to detect

Weaknesses

  • Repelled by strong light and certain anting-anting (amulets)
  • Disoriented by salt or ash barriers
  • Some myths say it fears its own reflection

Cultural Significance

The Sigbin plays a mysterious role in Filipino folklore. Some tales portray it as a deadly predator, while others claim that influential families harbor them for supernatural aid. Its legend underscores themes of secrecy, fear of the unknown, and the interplay between wealth and the occult in Philippine society.
