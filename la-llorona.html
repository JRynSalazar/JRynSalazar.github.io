La Llorona


Overview: La Llorona, or "The Weeping Woman," is a ghostly figure from Latin American and Filipino folklore. She is known for wandering near bodies of water, crying out for her lost children. The story of La Llorona varies, but she is often depicted as a tragic figure who drowned her children in a fit of madness or grief.

P hysical Description

  • Appears as a woman dressed in white, with long, flowing hair
  • Her face is often obscured, and she is seen crying or wailing
  • Her footsteps are said to echo in the night as she searches for her children

Abilities

  • Ability to cry loudly, heard from far distances
  • Haunting presence that instills fear and sadness
  • Can lure people near bodies of water, where she can drown them or claim their souls

Weaknesses

  • Believed to be unable to cross running water, such as rivers
  • May be deterred by religious symbols or prayers

Cultural Significance

La Llorona is often used in Filipino folklore as a cautionary tale for children, warning them to stay away from rivers and lakes at night. Her tragic story is a symbol of maternal loss, guilt, and the consequences of one’s actions.
