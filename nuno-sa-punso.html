Nuno sa Punso


Overview: The Nuno sa Punso (literally “Ancestor of the Mound”) is a dwarf-like nature spirit in Filipino folklore said to dwell in earthen anthills or termite mounds, called punso. It is known for being highly territorial and vengeful if disturbed.

P hysical Description

  • Appears as a very old, small man with a long beard and traditional clothes
  • Often hunchbacked and wrinkled
  • Usually invisible to most humans unless it chooses to reveal itself

Abilities

  • Can curse those who disrespect or disturb its mound
  • Inflicts mysterious illnesses, swelling, or misfortune
  • Can be appeased through rituals, offerings, or verbal apologies like “Tabi-tabi po”

Weaknesses

  • Respects polite people who ask permission before passing by
  • Can be countered by traditional healers (albularyo) with rituals
  • May be vulnerable to certain herbs or anting-anting (amulets)

Cultural Significance

The Nuno sa Punso is a reflection of indigenous Filipino values around respect for nature and the unseen world. Children are taught to say “Tabi-tabi po” when passing by isolated mounds or quiet places, symbolizing deference to spirits and ancestral beings. The Nuno is a guardian of its space and a reminder that all actions—even in nature—should be done with respect.
